A timeline of resilience, from a collapsed dream to a community reborn.

The Ghost of Shirley

For ten years, the school sat in silence after the 2013 collapse. What was once a place of laughter, a vibrant center for early childhood development became a ghost of the community‚Äôs hopes. Our journey began here‚Äînot with a budget, but with a scrappy laptop and a vision to bring life back to these walls and reclaim this space for the next generation.

Hard Work & Real Results

Revitalization required more than just funding; it required grit. Securing a grant early 2024 from the National Development Agency was just the dawn of the long anticipated new beginning. It boosted the real transformation that happened on the ground, led by local hands. From clearing decade-old thickets and dangerous trees threatening the structure to securing water through a new borehole, we didn't just paint walls; we rebuilt a sanctuary.

"Every person who held a panga or a spade here didn't just clear grass; they cleared a path for our children."

The Reason We Fight

This is why it matters. In 2025, we saw our first small group of graduates walk through these doors. A living proof that resilience pays off, but the work is fragile. We are building not just a school, but a sustainable future. Though enrolment is small, the impact is massive. These faces are evidence that Shirley Pre-School is back‚Äîand we aren't done yet.

The work isn't finished. The grass is high, and the mission is far from over.
